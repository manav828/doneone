DoneOne is designed to give you that time back. It is not just a to-do list; it is a dedicated command center for your squad.

In this guide, we’ll show you how to set up a high-performance team environment in under 10 minutes.

## Phase 1: Claiming Your Territory (Setup)

Unlike Company Owners who worry about "Billing" and "Legal Entities," you care about The Work. DoneOne understands this distinction.

### 1. The Fork in the Road
When you sign up, simply select: "I manage a Team."
Use this if you are leading a specific unit (e.g., "Frontend Squad") within a larger organization, or if you are piloting DoneOne for your group.

### 2. Naming Your Unit
You don't need to define the whole company hierarchy. Just name your team.
*   *Example:* "Mobile App Team" or "Q4 Growth Squad".

Result: You get a clean, focused workspace dedicated solely to your team's output. No noise from HR or Sales.

## Phase 2: Defining the Playbook (Departments & Roles)

Even a small team needs structure. In your Admin Panel, you define how *your* team operates.

1.  Departments (Sub-Teams): If you manage a cross-functional squad, create buckets like *iOS*, *Android*, and *QA*.
2.  Roles: Who is who? Define *Lead Dev*, *Senior Designer*, and *Junior Tester*.

This isn't just paperwork—it controls permissions later. A "Junior" might not be able to delete projects, keeping your data safe.

## Phase 3: Drafting the Players (Onboarding)

You don't have time to manually create accounts for everyone.

The Manager's Fast Track:
1.  Copy your Team Join Code.
2.  Drop it in your team Slack channel: "Join our new board here."
3.  As they pop up in your Pending Requests, click Approve.

Pro Tip: Assign them to their specific "Department" (e.g., iOS) immediately. This makes assigning work later lightning fast.

---

## Phase 4: The War Room (Project Execution)

This is where you will live. The "Project" is your unit of delivery.

### 1. Creating the Mission
Click "New Project" and stop starting from scratch.
*   Running Sprints? Use the *Agile Template* (Backlog -> Todo -> In Progress -> Code Review -> Done).
*   Launching a Feature? Use the *Product Launch Template*.

### 2. Staffing the Mission
This is the superpower of DoneOne. You don't dump the whole team into every project.
*   For "iPhone X Update", filter your team by "iOS Department".
*   Select only the 2 devs and 1 QA needed.

Why this matters: Your Android devs never see this project. Their sidebar stays clean, and they stay focused on their own work.

## Phase 5: The Daily Standup (Oversight)

Forget the hour-long Zoom call. You can run your team by looking at the Reports Dashboard for 5 minutes.

*   The Bottleneck Check: Look at the "Status Distribution" chart. Is 60% of work sitting in "Code Review"? You know exactly where to intervene.
*   The Burnout Check: Check the "Workload" chart. Is your Lead Designer assigned 15 active tasks while others have 2? Rebalance the load with a few clicks.
