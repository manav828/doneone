## Phase 1: The Foundation (Registration & Setup)

The first step is establishing your digital headquarters. Unlike other tools that force you into a generic "workspace," DoneOne asks you who you are and builds the structure for you.

### 1. Naming Your Headquarters
Whether you are an Owner or a Manager, everything starts with the Company Name. This creates your secure container.
*   *Example:* "Apex Digital" or "Creative Squad A".

### 2. The Architecture: Owners vs. Managers
Here is where the magic happens. DoneOne adapts the onboarding flow based on your role:

*   For Agency Owners: You likely have a complex hierarchy. During setup, you can create Multiple Departments immediately (e.g., "Design", "Development", "Marketing", "HR").
*   For Managers: You might only lead one unit. You start by creating One Department (e.g., "Frontend Team"). *Pro Tip: If your responsibilities grow, you can always add more departments later from the Admin Panel.*

## Phase 2: Structuring Your Workforce (The Admin Panel)

Once you're in, it’s time to mirror your real-world organization. Don't dump everyone into one big list.

### Departments & Roles
Go to the **Admin Panel** to fine-tune your setup.
1.  Departments: Create a "bucket" for every team. If you are a full-service agency, you might have *SEO*, *PPC*, and *Copywriting*.
2.  Roles: Define who holds power. Create custom roles like "Senior Editor" (who can approve work) vs. "Intern" (who can only draft).

## Phase 3: Frictionless Onboarding (The Join Code)

Stop manually inviting people one by one. DoneOne uses a **Smart Join Code** system.

1.  Copy the Code: Grab your unique Company ID (e.g., `APEX-8821`) from the settings.
2.  Slack it to your team: "Hey everyone, join our new workspace here using this code."
3.  Gatekeeping: New sign-ups appear in your "Pending Requests" queue. You approve them and instantly slot them into their correct Department and Role.

## Phase 4: The Project Engine (Execution)

Now that your people are set, let's look at the work.

### The Problem with "Noise"
In most tools, a developer sees *every* project, including HR's "Hiring Pipeline" and Marketing's "Social Calendar." That is clutter.

### The DoneOne Solution: Department-Based Assignment
When you create a new project (e.g., "Nike Web Rebrand"):
1.  Select Template: Choose "Web Development" (with pre-built columns like *Backlog*, *QA*, *Deploy*).
2.  Assign Resources: Filter by "Development Department".
3.  Select Staff: Pick only the 3 developers working on this.

Result: The Marketing team *never even sees* this project. Their dashboard remains clean. Your developers stay focused.
