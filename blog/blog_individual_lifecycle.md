## Phase 2: Segmentation (Clients vs. Life)

The biggest mistake freelancers make is mixing "Client Work" with "Buy Milk" in a single to-do list. That creates anxiety.

### The DoneOne Strategy: Separate Projects
Create distinct projects for the different "modes" of your brain.

1.  Project A: "Client Work"
    *   *Template:* Kanban (To Do -> In Progress -> Waiting on Client -> Invoiced).
    *   *Why:* Keeps your billable hours visible and trackable.
2.  Project B: "Life Admin"
    *   *Template:* Simple List.
    *   *Why:* A quick place to dump thoughts like "Call Dentist" or "Pay Utility Bill" so you can focus back on work.

## Phase 3: The Productivity Engine (Execution)

Now that you are organized, how do you actually work faster?

### 1. The Timer (Your Billing Best Friend)
Stop guessing how long a task took.
*   Open a task card.
*   Click the Play ▶ button.
*   DoneOne tracks every second. At the end of the month, just check the log to invoice your client accurately.

### 2. "My Tasks" View
You probably work across 3-4 different projects. Don't click into each one.
*   Click "My Tasks" to see an aggregated list of *everything* due today, sorted by priority.

## Phase 4: Scaling Up (The "Guest Star" Feature)

Sometimes, you *do* need to collaborate. Maybe you hired a subcontractor for a week, or a client wants to see progress.

You don't need to create a Company.
1.  Go to your "Web Design" project.
2.  Click "Share".
3.  Invite your client as a "Guest".

Result: They only see *that specific project*. They cannot see your "Life Admin" project or your other clients. You look professional, and your data stays private.
